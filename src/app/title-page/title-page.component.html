About this website

This website serves as an interface designed specifically to access a spelling database created from The Linguistic Atlas of Early Middle English (LAEME). The core feaure of the database is that searches can target word segments (litterae). Basic searches include:

  • List all the alternatives of a selected littera
  • List all lexels (lemmata) where a given grapheme alternates with others
  • Similarly to LAEME, the interface includes construction of maps.

Apart from these simple searches the interface allows you to display "profiles" of individual texts and compare them.

This website is under development and is not yet publicly accessible.

About LAEME

LAEME was designed as a research tool enabling access to information on Early Middle English texts and dialects. It was published in electronic form and is accessible online. Its basis is a corpus of Early Middle English texts. The size of the corpus is 650,000 words and it has detailed lexico-grammatical tagging as well as information about the original manuscripts. A searchable index of sources and information about the individual manuscripts are also available.
