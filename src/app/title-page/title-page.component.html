Poema Morale

Linguistic Atlas of Early Middle English

This webpage presents a digital edition of the early Middle English verse sermon Poema Morale, extant in seven copies. It was built based on data available in the Linguistic Atlas of Early Middle English, which is a corpus of tagged and lemmatized eME texts. The corpus data was further processed to allow a more detailed and effective analysis of spelling.

About the Poema Morale

Poema Morale is a verse sermon written in the first person and the author is an unknown preacher. The preaching poet regrets that he has wasted his life in idleness and has not followed the path leading to heaven. He describes specific human sins and failures and contrasts them to the right behaviour which pleases God. He also tells the audience about God, His generosity and appreciation of good intentions and the effort to fulfil God’s will. Finally, the preaching poet speaks about Doomsday and the Last Judgement as well as the suffering in Hell, providing advice on how to lead a good Christian life and escape damnation.

About LAEME

LAEME was designed as a research tool enabling access to information on Early Middle English texts and dialects. It was published in electronic form and is accessible online. Its basis is a corpus of Early Middle English texts. The size of the corpus is 650,000 words and it has detailed lexico-grammatical tagging as well as information about the original manuscripts. A searchable index of sources and information about the individual manuscripts are also available.
